Jared Rewerts

I'm a Computer Engineer at the City of Edmonton. Open Source is the new frontier for municipalities, and my team is at the forefront. New business models are emerging and Engineers that build relationships within the right communities are what power them.

Rather than focusing on fullstack, frontend or backend, I focus on products. Good technology will always underpin good products, and whether I need to build a React front end, a scalable REST service or an Android app, as long as I'm passionate about the product, I can get it done.

Alright, let's talk tech.

YouCanBenefit, a citizen benefits navigator. Built entirely in the open by the BetaCityYEG community, I've helped streamline the development effort. Our goal is to make it fun to contribute engineering hours to something that has a massive impact on your community. Built with an Angular frontend, a NestJS backend and Elasticsearch - containerized and hosted in Kubernetes.

PedCo, a privacy-friendly pedestrian counter with a sleek, 3d printed housing. Built to alleviate privacy concerns, this tiny detector uses edge-computed thermal pedestrian counting to minimize both cost and privacy invasion. Detection results are live streamed to Open Data for good measure. Uses OpenCV for blob detection and LoRa for long range, low energy communication.

Mouse events are detected, so not only does it save crazy amounts of bandwidth, but it runs like a normal browser. Lynx, your time is near! Jazz, a live chat VIP support application. Built for Android, iOS and Chrome users, my team made sure this app would run anywhere our VIP clients would want it to. Built with a true product-driven focus, engineers, designers and subject matter experts worked collaboratively to solve a real problem.

Mailman: more emails than you've ever wanted to send, sent more easily than you've ever imagined. Born out of the need for a more powerful way to send emails from a Google Sheet, Mailman makes incredibly complicated merges easy using standard Google Sheets functions. Built with ES6 Javascript and Material Design Lite.

CBRAV. An aerial video processing platform that identifies human-human and human-vehicle interactions. Built while in the R&D department of MDA. Our work was presented at a conference called the international society for optics and photonics (SPIE).
